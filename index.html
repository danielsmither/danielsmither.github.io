Gongsmithing in Bali

While I was living in Bali, Indonesia I made a few trips to the village of Tihingan, Klungkung to make a few videos of the process to make some of the instruments in a Balinese gamelan. With the help of my teacher Pak I Wayan Rajeg and my friend, Komang, I was able to get an up close look at how they take discs of raw bronze and form it into the gorgeous instruments of a gong kebyar. Check out how the gangsa keys and a reyong pot is made in my videos to the right. Also, notice the lack of safety equipment and even proper shoes!

Studying Gamelan in Bali

In addition to investigating how the instruments were made, I spent a great deal of my time in Bali studying the music of the gong kebyar and especially gender wayang ensemble. When I left Indonesia, I had a plethora of recordings and videos to take with me. I spent most of my time studying with my teacher I Ketut Candra at his home in central Denpasar. You can view polos and sangsih separated videos that I made of a piece for sad scenes during a wayang called "Mesem." My teacher, Ketut is playing.
