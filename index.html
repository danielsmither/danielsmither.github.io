Projects

Gongsmithing in Bali

While I was living in Bali, Indonesia I made a few trips to the village of Tihingan, Klungkung to make a few videos of the process to make some of the instruments in a Balinese gamelan. With the help of my teacher Pak I Wayan Rajeg and my friend, Komang, I was able to get an up close look at how they take discs of raw bronze and form it into the gorgeous instruments of a gong kebyar. Check out how the gangsa keys and a reyong pot is made in my videos to the right. Also, notice the lack of safety equipment and even proper shoes!

Studying and Documenting Gamelan in Bali

In addition to investigating how the instruments were made, I spent a great deal of my time in Bali studying the music of the gong kebyar and especially gender wayang ensemble. When I left Indonesia, I had a plethora of recordings and videos to take with me. I spent most of my time studying with my teacher I Ketut Candra at his home in central Denpasar. You can view polos and sangsih separated videos that I made of a piece for sad scenes during a wayang called "Mesem." My teacher, Ketut, is playing.

Langlang Ulang

Langlang Ulang is a gender wayang piece I learned from my teacher in Bali. As part of my final project for my B.A. at VCU, I transcribed part of this piece and then created a lead sheet of it arranged for piano and vibraphone. You can see my transcription and lead sheet by clicking on the buttons below. The videos are from my lessons and are the recordings I used to transcribe it.

Performances

Tour of Bali

In 2010, I had the opportunity to join a mini tour of Bali with Richmond, Virginia's Gamelan Raga Kusuma. We opened for Gamelan Dharma Swara from New York City at performances in the villages of Tulikup, Gianyar and Kerambitan, Tabanan. To the right is a video of our performance in Tulikup. I am in the center, playing ugal.

Fat Cat

Occasionally, I've also played with Gamelan Raga Kusuma at various locations in the USA ranging from the Indonesian Embassy in Washington, D.C. to basement bars in New York City's East Village, such as Fat Cat. To the right is a video of us playing the music for Tari Kembang Girang, an interesting piece played on gender wayang instruments using the technique for playing gong kebyar instruments.

Bali Ha'i -- Miami, Florida

In March of 2017, along with Danny Martin, I was asked to play gender wayang music for the annual fundraiser for the Kampong, a national tropical botanical garden located in Miami, Florida. Here is a short clip of us playing part of the gineman (opening) of Sulendro, a famous petegak or "sitting piece" played throughout Bali. Here, we perform it in the Sukawati style.

Wayang Sutasoma -- Richmond, Virginia

In March of 2020, I joined members of Gamelan Raga Kusuma as well as master dalang and musician, I Gusti Putu Sudarta in a performance of his Wayang Sutasoma. We performed this for both Wake Forest University and Yale University. Below is a link to the YouTube video of the performance for Yale University. Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, we were unable to travel to New Haven, Connecticut to perform live and Gusti had to return to Bali prematurely. As such, we streamed the performance from our rehearsal space in Richmond, Virginia.
